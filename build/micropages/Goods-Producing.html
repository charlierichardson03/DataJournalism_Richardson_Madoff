Analysis

The goods production industry has been on the steady decline from 1998 to 2011. Interestingly enough, the dot com crash did not really affect the goods production industry. In fact, from 1998 to 2001 the industry actually strengthened. This may be because not many of the internet companies that went bankrupt during the crisis were actually based in New York, despite providing their services to its citizens. The decline beginning in 2001 was most likely caused by the closure of many of New York City’s factories. The industry never recovered to its 1999 levels despite its climb starting in 2011, with its employment rate in 2020 being only 76% of the employment rate in 1998. The industry was hit hard during the beginning of the pandemic but has since recovered to about 88% of what it was. This industry follows the trend that many other industries follow, with its employment numbers generally following the different crises.
