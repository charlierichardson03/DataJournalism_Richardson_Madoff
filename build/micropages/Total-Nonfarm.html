Analysis

The unemployment numbers in Total Nonfarm only bump up in two places: around 2001 and around 2008. These two dips are explained by the economic crisis of those times. In the early 2000s, the dot com crash hit the tech sector hard. In 2007 and 2008, the housing sector crashed, resulting in the near collapse of the economy. The unemployment rates then recovered starting in 2009, increasing 27% between 2009 and 2020. Total Nonfarm makes up a large piece of the total unemployment in New York City because it contains the largest category of industries within it.
